Goat Classification and Morphology (Capra aegagrus) - Goats are one of the livestock belonging to small ruminants, which have long been cultivated and farmed in Indonesia. This goat is estimated to come from West Asia, but also said that this goat came from Europe. Goats spread rapidly to various regions and provinces because the growth and development of these goats was very fast. Goats also include mamabiak animals that can easily adapt easily in new environments.

In addition, there are several studies that this goat can be classified according to its level and morphology based on its shape and varietes. This can be done by looking at the classification and the morphology as follows.

Classification of goats

Kingdom: Animalia

Phylum: Chordata

Class: Mammals

Order: Artiodactyla

Family: Bovidae

Sub family: caprinae

Genus: Capra

Species: Capra aegagrus

Sub species: Capra aegagrus hircus

Data source: Linnaeus, 1758: 32

Goat morphology

   Goats generally have two horns on the left and right

   Medium-sized goat

   Male weight reaches 120 kg and 50 kg female is even more dependent on the varietes

   It has colors ranging from black, white striped black, white black, and also brownish in color.

   Has a short, average tail measuring 2-3 cm

   Has a beard with a length of 3-4 cm

   Convex forehead, and flat nose
