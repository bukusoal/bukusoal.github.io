3. contoh kalimat pronoun : subject pronoun, object pronoun, possessive pronoun, replexive possessive, replexive pronoun​


Pronoun is a word that takes the place of a noun, such as: I, you, me, it, they, we, she, him, us. There are several types of pronouns: subjective, objective, possessive adjectives, and possessive pronouns.

Pembahasan

Contoh kalimat dengan subject pronoun yaitu

I have a cat.

Contoh kalimat dengan object pronoun yaitu

A cat sleeps with me everyday.

Contoh kalimat dengan possessive pronoun yaitu

That cat is mine.

Contoh kalimat dengan reflexive pronoun yaitu

My brother can study himself.

Contoh kalimat dengan adjective possessive yaitu

My cat has long black tail.

Pada soal tertulis "reflexive possessive", tetapi tidak ada pronoun dalam kategori ini. Yang ada adalah possessive pronoun dan adjective possessive.

10. PRONOUN (kata ganti benda / orang) sebagai 1. Subject 2. Object 3. Possessive adjective 4. Possessive pronoun Selanjutnya huat contoh​


Penjelasan:

Pronoun (kata ganti) memiliki berbagai peran dalam kalimat, termasuk sebagai subject, object, possessive adjective, dan possessive pronoun. Berikut adalah contoh-contoh penggunaan pronoun dalam berbagai peran:

1. Subject Pronoun (Kata Ganti Subjek):

- I (saya): I am going to the store.

- You (kamu/anda): You are my friend.

- He (dia laki-laki): He is a doctor.

- She (dia perempuan): She is singing beautifully.

- It (untuk benda atau hewan tanpa jenis kelamin): It is a cat.

- We (kami): We are studying together.

- They (mereka): They are playing in the park.

2. Object Pronoun (Kata Ganti Objek):

- Me (saya): He called me yesterday.

- You (kamu/anda): I see you at the party.

- Him (dia laki-laki): I like him.

- Her (dia perempuan): She helped her friend.

- It (untuk benda atau hewan tanpa jenis kelamin): The book is heavy; I can't lift it.

- Us (kami): Please join us for dinner.

- Them (mereka): I met them at the conference.

3. Possessive Adjective (Kata Ganti Kepemilikan):

- My (milik saya): This is my car.

- Your (milik kamu/anda): Is this your pen?

- His (milik dia laki-laki): That is his house.

- Her (milik dia perempuan): Her book is on the table.

- Its (untuk benda atau hewan tanpa jenis kelamin): The cat is washing its paws.

- Our (milik kami): Our school is big.

- Their (milik mereka): This is their project.

4. Possessive Pronoun (Kata Ganti Kepemilikan):

- Mine (milik saya): The red bike is mine.

- Yours (milik kamu/anda): The keys are yours.

- His (milik dia laki-laki): The blue jacket is his.

- Hers (milik dia perempuan): The bag is hers.

- Its (untuk benda atau hewan tanpa jenis kelamin): The book is old, but its pages are still intact.

- Ours (milik kami): The house is ours.

- Theirs (milik mereka): The decision is theirs.

Penggunaan pronoun dalam kalimat membantu untuk menghindari pengulangan kata-kata dan membuat kalimat lebih ringkas dan jelas.

13. tolong buatkan soal pronoun(subjektive, Objektive, possessive adjektive) pakai a,b,c,d beserta jawabannya


Explanation:

The pronoun is a word which is used to replace a noun. The pronoun can be placed as subject and object.
There are many kinds of pronouns but I will explain 5 of them which is related to 1st person (i, we), 2nd person (you), and 3rd person (he, she, it, they) only.

1. Subject personal pronoun
This pronoun is used as a subject in a sentence.
It consists of I, you, he, she, it, we, and they

2.  Object personal pronoun
This pronoun is used as an object in a sentence.
It consists of me, you, him, her, it, us, and them

3. Possessive adjective pronoun
This pronoun shows possession to a noun and it is used as an adjective in a sentence.
It consists my, your, his, her, its, our, and their

4. Possessive pronoun
This pronoun shows possession of a noun and it is used as a pronoun because it can be placed as subject or object.
It consists mine, yours, his, hers, ours, and theirs

5. Reflexive pronoun
This pronoun is used when the subject's action influences the subject itself.
It consists myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, and themselves.
