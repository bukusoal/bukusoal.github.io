Translate to English :

Quoted from the Okezone.com article, this article discusses the rainy season, Bogor residents are asked to be prepared for disasters.

BOGOR - Deputy Regent of Bogor Iwan Setiawan appealed to the public to be prepared for disasters. That's because almost all areas of Bogor Regency are prone to disasters during the rainy season. This was said by Iwan when holding a Disaster Preparedness Appeal which also coincided with Heroes' Day in Cibinong, Bogor Regency, Wednesday (10/11/2021). "The contours of the Bogor Regency area are diverse. Hills, many rivers flow, it's no wonder when the rainy season comes, disasters often occur. So we all have to be on alert," said Iwan. So far, the Bogor Regency Government has begun to carry out a number of disaster mitigation measures. This includes building a retaining wall (TPT). carry out massive socialization to the community to stay alert every time it rains. "We also carry out socialization and education on disaster preparedness. We use social media as a means to disseminate information to the community. Hopefully there will be no major disaster," he said.

Meanwhile, Head of BPBD Bogor Regency Yani Hassan said his party had made disaster mitigation efforts. Including continuing to coordinate with a number of other relevant agencies. During this rainy season, all sub-districts in Bogor Regency have the potential for disasters such as floods, landslides or tornadoes. The Rapid Response Team (TRC) will always act quickly to anticipate and manage disasters.

"The TRC has 105 personnel who are divided into three teams. They continue to be on standby to anticipate disasters that occur, especially now that the rainy season has started," he said.

Yani added, BPBD has also installed CCTV in several locations that are considered flood-prone. So that it can minimize the impact or victims who become flood customers in Bogor Regency. "This year we have received assistance from eight CCTVs that have been installed in vulnerable locations. Because in Bogor there are quite a number of areas that are prone to flooding, including Cibinong District," he said. Then for landslide areas, BPBD installed sensor poles in several locations such as Sukajaya, Cigudeg and others. In case of ground movement, officers can monitor and anticipate. "With this pole, we can monitor it, by looking at the sensor pole. If the pole continues to move, information will come to us for us to continue as an early anticipation," said Yani.
